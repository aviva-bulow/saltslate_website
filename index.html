Our Mission

We design nutritious food that fits within a low income budget.
We help people plan, shop, and cook so that they save time, save money, and eat better.

Save Time


  • Get a new menu each month that's tailored to your household's government benefits, allergies, food restrictions, and tastes.

  • Use recipes that are designed as a group to maximize your efficiency.

  • Get customized shopping lists and detailed cooking instructions.


    • According to the USDA, SNAP and WIC participants spend more time cooking than any other demographic in the US.
    By helping people shop and cook more efficiently, we reduce their stress and improve their lives.


Save Money


  • Get delicious, seasonal recipes that fit within your budget.

  • Maximize your cash benefits, and don't run out before the month is over.

  • Get menus that account for all your benefits including WIC, FDPIR, and school lunches or breakfasts.


    • To live off of a SNAP budget, participants can afford to spend $1.50 per person per meal.
    Nearly all SNAP participants use up most of their food benefits by the middle of the month.


Eat Better


  • Take care of yourself and your family with healthy food.

  • We'll help you create nutritional goals for yourself and for each member of your household.

  • Get recipes and tips that are designed to help you meet your daily, weekly, and monthly goals.


    • While malnourishment-related ailments declined overall in US since 1990, they've increased in the lowest socioeconomic class.
    By helping people eat more healthfully we can reduce rates of diabetes, obesity, dementia, cancer, and more.

Why We Do It

Nearly one in every three households in the United States is low income.

People in this group spend more time cooking than any other demographic, have increased levels of food insecurity, and are more malnourished than the rest of the population.

Malnourishment


Children in the low income bracket are highly likely to have nutritionally inadiquate diets. %69 do not eat the minimum recommended amount of meat each day, %94 do not consume the minimum recommended amount of vegitables, and nearly %100 don't meet the minimum recommended amount of dairy.

In at least one study, %40 of low income individuals of all ages were deficient in at least one of vitamins A, B6, B12, C, D, E, folate, or iron. In comparison, those with "high-income" (%350 of poverty) were about half as likely to have any deficiencies in this group.

Lower income individuals are nearly twice as likely to be obese when compaired to their high income counterparts. In fact, metobolic syndrome in general is higly corrilated with food insufficiency.


Food Insecurity


37.2 million people in the US suffered from food insecurity in 2018, 6 million of them were children.

Food insecurity is corillated with depression in women.

It increases rates of domestic violence in households without mental health or substance abuse problems.

It causes metobolic syndrome, and it results in malnourishment.


Stress


SNAP and WIC participants spend more time cooking than almost any other demographic in the US.

For those living in poverty, time is a scarce resource.
Travel is often on public transit, or on foot. Food is often cooked at home. In short: life takes more time.

Spending hours each week to design healthy, low cost meals is not feasible.

We help people plan, shop, and cook nutritious food in less time.
This frees them up for other tasks, so they needn't sacrifice their health on the alter of their responsibilities.
